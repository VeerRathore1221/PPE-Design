All About PPE

What is PPE?

Personal Protective equipment, also referred to as PPE, is equipment/clothing worn to protect yourself or others from hazards that may cause injury in the workplace. Personal protective quipment includes gloves, shoe covers, masks, gowns, and face shields.


Different types of PPE

Gown

The gown protects the wearer from physical contact
and reduce the transmission of harmful microorganisms
from coming in contact with the wearer such as the patient coughing, or sneezing on the gown.
This is worn when the patient is on droplet, contact, and airborne precaution.
They break the chain of infection at Mode of Transmission.



Gloves

The gloves protect the wearer from physical contact when touching the patient.
It prevents harmful bacteria from coming in contact with the hands when touching the patient, inspecting their torso, mouth, genitals, etc.
They break the chain of infection at Mode of Transmission. This is worn when the patient is on droplet, airborne, or contact precaution.



Mask/Face Shield

The mask protects the wearer from airborne bacteria and keeps it from entering the mouth or nose.
Masks include N95s, KN95s, surgical N95s, cloth masks, or face shields.
It is worn for droplet or airborne precaution.
It breaks the Chain of Infection at Portal of Entry or Portal of Exit.



Shoe Covers

Shoe Covers are worn to keep your shoes clean and disinfected thorugh keeping away bacteria that is on the ground.
If the patient threw up, the doctor would accordingly wear shoe covers to be able to go into the room safely.
The shoe covers break the chain of infection at Mode of Transmission.
They are worn if the patient is on droplet precaution.

Order For Donning and Doffing



Donning:

1. Don the gown first

2. Wear the mask

3. Wear the goggles/faceshield

3. Wear hair cover

4. Wear shoe cover

5. Lastly, wear the gloves



Doffing:

1. Take one glove off, grasping the palm area of the glove.
Then, ball up the glove in one hand, and with your gloveless hand, making sure not to touch the outside of the glove, take the glove off with the glove balled up in your hand and take it off, balling it up in a ball.
Dispose of it in the trash.

2. Take the goggles off from the back by lifting it up above your ears holding it by the headband.
Reusable material can be reused after disinfection.
Disposable items should be disposed of.

3. Without letting the sleeves touch your body, unfasten the ties and grab the inside of the handle.
Ball the gown up in a ball and dispose of it.

4. Remove both ties from each ear, making sure not to touch the front of the mask.
Dispose of it.

5. Remove hair and shoe covers by grabbing the inside of the cuff and pulling out.
Ball it up from the inside and ispose of them both.
